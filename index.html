About Me

I am a third-year student at the Faculty of Electrical Engineering here in Sarajevo. I am a passionate Android App developer.

Ever since I was a little kid I loved computers. And after my childhood friend once showed me how to make a game using this software called Gamemaker, and how you could actually make a visual calculator in this bulky weird studio using Visual Basic, a language that I never heard of before, that love just amplifies more for me. I knew I wanted to know more about it.

In high school, on the other hand, in 3rd grade, every programming class I attended, I couldn’t ignite the fire in me to go forward. So many questions. Why are we learning this old programming language called Pascal? What's the point of this black and white console? Why are we doing some basic math problems? Is this in any sense connected to real programming? My undeveloped mind just didn’t give it a second thought. And then a miracle happened.

My high school teacher informed me that Academy387 was offering this free 7-day course on Ruby and Ruby on Rails. And it was a life-changer. We learned how to make games like Flappy bird, Tic Tac Toe, and it wasn't about the games that ignite this spark of interest in me, it was that connection with reality. That year, for my graduation project I created this C# desktop application called Medicom. It was about enabling doctors and patients to communicate directly. It was inspired by enormous everyday crowds in our local hospital. I was very pleased with the way the app came out.

The faculty of computer science I was attending, and that I am still attending didn't do much for me either. The pressure was too high and my creativity and willingness to explore different programming fields dropped down pretty fast. Until I had this "Mobile app development" course, where I developed my first android app in Java. I was in love again.

Even though I was scared of change, mainly because I loved Java, following the advice of my friend, I started learning Kotlin. And for 4 months I was trying to understand as much as I could, basically studying top recommended books, reading endless amounts of many Kotlin/Android blogs in my free time... I am not gonna lie. It was difficult and overwhelming, but I finally delivered. I design and made my two respectable apps that represents my overall knowledge of Android development. Honestly, after developing in Kotlin, Java just looks like one of those exes that you are questioning yourself: "Was she prettier when we dated or what?". So much boilerplate.

And this is what describes me the most. Eagerness to learn more and more about programming is deeply rooted in my personality. Taking a tremendous amount of my free time to learn Android and still perform well on my exams is proving what I am just stating. And why Android development is my choice? Well, the fact that you can work on something and the result of your hard work is in the palm of your hand, just amazes me.

VodovodInfoApp (Android/Kotlin App)

This is an app that collects water outage notices from Sarajevo's government water supply company, locates areas on the map and notifies users accordingly.

This app uses:
  • MVVM architectural pattern
  • Jsoup (Java HTML parser) to scrape data from viksa.ba
  • Repository pattern as a single source of truth
  • LiveData to appropriately notifies Presentation (UI) layer accordingly to observers livecycle.
  • Google geolocate API to locate streets mentioned in notices
  • Google maps to display the data
  • Room persistence library is used to manage the data retrieval
  • Hilt is used to provides a standard way to do DI injection
  • Hilt and periodic Worker for local notifications
